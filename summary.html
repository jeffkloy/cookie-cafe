Market Value

The "Market Value" of a thing is the price that somebody would pay for that thing in a competitive economy.

If the average cost of cookies of your competitors is $3.00, then $3.00 is the current market value.

Does Market Value change?

Yes! It changes according to how valueable the product is. Let's say, there was a crazy insect infestation that made all of the cocoa trees sick, therefore making chocolate hard to produce since there would be no cocoa to work with. This makes chocolate chip cookies more valuable because it's harder to purchase chocolate in general, and thus the market value has increased.
